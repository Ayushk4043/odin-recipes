Rajma Chawal Recipe

What Is Butter Chicken?

One of the most popular dishes in North India from Punjab. In this Punjabi rajma chawal delicacy, cooked kidney beans is excitingly flavoured with a wide array of ingredients ranging from ginger and green chillies to tomatoes and onions, spices and masala powders too and then mixed with rice. However, you will be surprised to see that all these are everyday ingredients, which you do not have to go shopping for!

Ingredients

For the For Rajma

  • 2 cups cooked Red Kidney Beans or Rajma
  • 2 Tomatoes
  • 1 large Onion
  • 1-2 cloves Garlic
  • 1/2" piece of Ginger
  • 2 tsp Cumin Powder or Jeera powder
  • 1 tsp of Coriander Powder or Dhania Powder
  • 1/2 tsp of Garam Masala
  • 1 tsp of Cumin seeds or Jeera
  • 1 tsp Sugar or Jaggery powder
  • 1 tbsp of Kasuri Methi (Optional)
  • 4 tsp of Oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Water
  • A handful of coriander leaves for garnish

For the Chawal:

  • 2 cups of Water
  • 1 cup of Rice (preferably Basmati)

Recipe

  1. Wash the rice twice with sufficient water.
  2. In a deep pan, bring 2 cups of water to boil.
  3. Once it comes to a rolling boil, add the washed rice and cover and cook until the rice is done.
  4. Drain out any excess water.
  5. Alternatively, pressure cook the rice for around 2-3 whistles or
  6. 8-10 minutes after the pressure builds up to cut the rice
  7. cooking time into half.
  8. Heat the oil in a pan and add the cumin seeds.
  9. Grind together the onion, ginger and garlic and add to the pan.
  10. Fry the onion paste until it turns slightly brown or for about 3-4 minutes.
  11. Grind the tomato into a puree.
  12. Add the tomato puree to the pan and mix well. Fry for 2-3 minutes.
  13. Now add all the spice powders and 1 cup water and allow it to simmer on low heat for 4-5 minutes. If you used dried beans and cooked it yourself, then instead of plain water, you can use the water in which the beans were cooked in.
  14. Add the cooked beans, salt and sugar or jaggery and cook for another 2-3 minutes.
  15. Add the kasuri methi and mix well.
  16. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve with rice.
